Augmented Reality

As part of an agency project, a team of six students from UConn's Digital Media and Design program were tasked with developing an innovative approach to visual learning for high school students. I was given the opportunity to work on two different versions of the project: 3DNA and DNA Transcription. For 3DNA, my responsibility was to design and develop the prototype and wireframe. Through this process, I aimed to create an engaging and user-friendly experience for the target audience. In addition, I worked on DNA Transcription, where my focus was to create an augmented reality environment for video placement. By incorporating this technology, we aimed to enhance the learning experience for high school students. Overall, this project allowed me to contribute to the development of a cutting-edge educational tool while honing my skills in design, prototyping, and augmented reality.

Align the Dot

I have developed a game using Processing that challenges players to guide a blue ball to align horizontally with a black ball located on the left side of the screen. The game's objective is to align the two balls and then quickly move the blue ball to the right by pressing the right arrow key, followed by dragging it off the screen. To play the game, simply click on the canvas, and the game will start. My main goal in developing this game was to create an entertaining and engaging experience for players, while also showcasing my coding skills and understanding of game design principles.

Orthosnap

During my time as an intern at the venture capital firm Loeb.nyc, I was paired up with one of their portfolio companies, OrthoSnap. My first assignment involved redesigning and developing OrthoSnap's internal website, with the aim of improving its functionality and user experience. After successfully completing the website project, I was given the task of creating a program for OrthoSnap's quality control system. This program was designed to highlight the key differences in impressions from patients, in order to improve the assessment of the patient's next adjustments for their clear aligners. By implementing this program, OrthoSnap was able to enhance its quality control measures, ultimately leading to better patient outcomes. Overall, my internship with Loeb.nyc and OrthoSnap allowed me to gain valuable experience in website development, quality control systems, and project management. I am grateful for the opportunity to have worked with such innovative companies and look forward to continuing to develop my skills in the tech industry.

WKNDR

About the project

WKNDR is a user-friendly web application that enables friends and families to discover exciting local experiences. Using the app, users can select their interests and preferences, and the AI-powered search engine generates personalized recommendations based on Google search results. One of the key features of WKNDR is its curated list of experiences, which helps to alleviate the common problem of analysis paralysis. Instead of being overwhelmed with a long list of search results, users can browse a carefully selected list of activities and events that are tailored to their interests.

Problem

Weekend planning can be a complex and time-consuming process for many people. Whether you're spending time with your partner, friends, or family, it can be challenging to find new and unique experiences that everyone will enjoy. Through our research, we have identified three key reasons why this problem occurs:

  1. Analysis paralysis: Many people suffer from analysis paralysis, which is the inability to make a decision due to being overwhelmed with too many options.
  2. Spontaneity vs. planning: Some people prefer to be spontaneous and go with the flow, while others like to plan ahead and have a clear schedule of activities.
  3. Coordination challenges: Planning for a group can be challenging, as it requires time and energy to coordinate everyone's schedules and preferences.
